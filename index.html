Clinton Jenkins - ICT Manager

The kind of work done by an ICT Manager varies depending on how big the company is, whether there are other IT workers underneath this role. Generally, they are responsible for all day-to-day IT operations. They maintain, monitor, service and upgrade all sections of a company's IT related infrastructure. While also supporting and managing IT security.

An ICT Manager ensures a company's successful future and its demand for scalability when upgrading its IT systems. Working with the financial team of a company to keep its IT systems up to date, working out an annual budget, developing cost effective tenders to upgrade a company's tech and submitting these to the appropriate heads of department.

They are also responsible for all end users of an organisation, creating their login accounts, work emails, setting up their desk with all their required communication devices and displays. AV and media tech equipment for meeting/board rooms (i.e., Projectors with sound etc).

The ICT Manager must interact with everyone in the office. From delegating tasks to subordinates below them, to communicating with all non-IT related staff regarding any troubleshooting issues they may be suffering at the time. They also deal with all IT-related service providers (i.e., Phones, Internet, Cloud networking subscription services, etc).

The ICT Manager will spend a great deal of time at their desk if they’re lucky enough they could even have remote access to majority of a company's infrastructure. Thus, only requiring to be face-to-face for more upfront hands-on type jobs.

Some of their most challenging aspects include prioritizing of incoming day-to-day troubleshooting problems all the while trying to keep up with all other day-to-day maintenance of a company's IT infrastructure.

Raspberry Pi/Arduino are a type of single board computer (SBC). Originally designed to be a low-cost and simple open-source educational tool, they have expanded to a consumer market including industry hobbyists, prototype builders, gamers and the generally curious population with a huge variety of project applications with sensors and expansion boards. They also support a variety of operating systems and peripherals.
These low-cost computers introduced and paved the way for General Purpose Input-Output (GPIO) connection pins allowing it to be programmed for a vast range of different electronic components. Some of these components allow access to the internet for IoT based applications, others allow for customisable smart home solutions.

With a vast variety of expansion boards and sensors available the possibilities for an available low-cost solution for everyday projects. Some of the basic projects you can add to your smart home setup include but not limited to a smart light RGB LED light, door and window switch alarm, fire alarm/flame or Co2 alarms, temperature and humidity monitoring, light detection, water detection, soil moisture detection, infrared motion detection with intruder alarm. All these sensors can be programmed to log data, to notify/alert its user of certain scenes and automation algorithms and even connect to the IoT and receive alerts and updates direct to all devices.
The technological developments that can be attained using Raspberry Pi devices in the near future is only limited by the user who builds the product.

What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is an increasingly important issue in today’s digital world. Our lives are becoming increasingly intertwined with technology, from our banking and shopping needs to how we communicate with each other. If proper security measures are not implemented, all aspects of our lives could be vulnerable to cyber-attacks. In order to combat this, there has been a surge in technological developments designed to protect data from malicious actors.

State-of-the-art technology

The current state of the art of cybersecurity technology is one that depends on a mixture of technological solutions and user education. On the technological side, companies are investing in firewalls, encryption systems and intrusion detection software which can help protect data from unauthorized access and alert administrators when suspicious activity is detected. Furthermore, user training must take place so that employees are educated on how to spot potential threats, such as phishing emails or other instances of social engineering, as well as understand best practices for using new technologies like cloud computing or IoT devices securely.

What can be done now and, in the future

Looking ahead to what might become possible within the next three years, a number of exciting developments are being undertaken in this field. One innovation that is likely to be prominent is quantum computing; while still at an early stage of development, it promises speeds much faster than current computers allowing for faster and more secure processing of data – something which would further help boost cybersecurity efforts. Additionally, advancements in Artificial Intelligence (AI) may owe themselves to creating better models for detecting attacks before they can cause damage as AI algorithms learn over time how malicious actors operate.

The impact of these new technologies will be twofold; firstly, businesses will benefit by being able to secure their networks better against attack but also consumers will benefit from having their personal information safeguarded more effectively too. In terms of jobs, new roles focused on monitoring network security will likely become increasingly sought-after; alternatively, people skilled in developing or maintaining existing security systems may see increased career opportunities too due to the need for continual improvement and updating of existing technologies.

As far as everyday life goes then this should mean that people can feel safer when using technology whether through banking online or connecting with friends over messaging services – after all knowing your data is properly protected serves only as a comfort rather than an inconvenience! Furthermore, family members may also benefit either through having access to safer banking methods or simply providing assurance that their loved ones’ sensitive information isn’t at risk from malicious actors.

Technological/Developments that make this possible

One major technological development that has contributed to cybersecurity is the development of encryption algorithms. Encryption algorithms allow data to be transformed into a secure, unreadable form that can only be accessed by someone with the proper decryption key. This helps to protect data from being accessed or stolen by unauthorised individuals.

Another technological development that has made cybersecurity possible is the development of secure networks and systems. These networks and systems are designed to prevent unauthorised access and to detect and respond to security threats. This includes measures such as firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and antivirus software.

In addition to technological developments, there have also been other developments that have contributed to cybersecurity. One of these is the development of cybersecurity policies and guidelines. These policies and guidelines outline best practices for protecting systems and data from digital attacks, and they help organisations to develop effective cybersecurity strategies.

Another development that has contributed to cybersecurity is the emergence of cybersecurity professionals and organisations. These professionals and organisations are dedicated to researching, developing, and implementing cybersecurity measures, and they play a critical role in helping to protect systems and data from digital threats.

Overall, there have been many technological and other developments that have made cybersecurity possible. These developments have helped to protect systems, networks, and devices from digital attacks, theft, and damage, and they have played a vital role in ensuring the security and integrity of the digital world.

How will it impact us in the future Potential

The potential impact of the development of cybersecurity measures is significant, as it can help to protect individuals, organisations, and governments from a variety of digital threats. One of the key ways in which this impact is likely to be felt is through the increased security and reliability of the digital world. This could lead to a number of benefits, such as:
· Improved trust in online systems and services, as people feel more confident that their personal information and assets are secure
· Reduced financial loss and reputational damage for individuals and organisations, as the risk of cyber-attacks is reduced
· Increased security for critical infrastructure and sensitive information, which is important for the overall stability and security of society

It is likely that cybersecurity will continue to evolve and change in the future, as new technologies and threats emerge. Some of the changes that are likely to occur include:
· The increasing use of artificial intelligence and machine learning to improve cybersecurity: These technologies can be used to analyse vast amounts of data and identify patterns that might indicate a security threat. They can also be used to automate the process of detecting and responding to threats, making it easier to protect against cyber-attacks.
· The increasing use of biometric authentication: This involves using physical characteristics such as fingerprints, facial recognition, and iris scans to verify the identity of users. Biometric authentication can be more secure than traditional methods such as passwords, as it is more difficult to forge or steal biometric data.
· The increasing use of blockchain: Blockchain is a decentralised, secure database technology that is used to store and manage data. It is likely that blockchain will be increasingly used to secure data in the future, as it is difficult to hack and allows for the secure exchange of information without the need for intermediaries.
· The increasing need for cybersecurity professionals: As the digital world becomes more complex and vulnerable to cyber-attacks, there will be a growing demand for skilled cybersecurity professionals who can help to protect against these threats. This could lead to new job opportunities in the field of cybersecurity

Overall, it is likely that cybersecurity will continue to evolve and change in the future as new technologies and threats emerge. It will be important for individuals, organizations, and governments to stay up to date with these developments in order to stay protected against digital threats.

People that are affected and how

The ultimate goal of machine learning is to create systems that can learn and adapt on their own, without the need for human intervention or supervision.
