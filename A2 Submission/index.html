4.IT Work (Individual members reports with links etc)

Clinton Jenkins - ICT Manager

The kind of work done by an ICT Manager varies depending on how big the company is, whether there are other IT workers underneath this role. Generally, they are responsible for all day-to-day IT operations. They maintain, monitor, service and upgrade all sections of a company's IT related infrastructure. While also supporting and managing IT security.

An ICT Manager ensures a company's successful future and its demand for scalability when upgrading its IT systems. Working with the financial team of a company to keep its IT systems up to date, working out an annual budget, developing cost effective tenders to upgrade a company's tech and submitting these to the appropriate heads of department.

They are also responsible for all end users of an organisation, creating their login accounts, work emails, setting up their desk with all their required communication devices and displays. AV and media tech equipment for meeting/board rooms (i.e., Projectors with sound etc).

The ICT Manager must interact with everyone in the office. From delegating tasks to subordinates below them, to communicating with all non-IT related staff regarding any troubleshooting issues they may be suffering at the time. They also deal with all IT-related service providers (i.e., Phones, Internet, Cloud networking subscription services, etc).

The ICT Manager will spend a great deal of time at their desk if they’re lucky enough they could even have remote access to majority of a company's infrastructure. Thus, only requiring to be face-to-face for more upfront hands-on type jobs.

Some of their most challenging aspects include prioritizing of incoming day-to-day troubleshooting problems all the while trying to keep up with all other day-to-day maintenance of a company's IT infrastructure.

QA tester at Silverchip (software development company):

They work from home sometimes and sometimes go to the office. A day usually involves catching up on e-mails, looking through reports to prioritise the day. On Mondays they have a stand-up meting to see if any developers have issues or any blockers that need testing after campaign. After checking through the geo board, they work from, any urgent or high priority tickets will be tackled. They also need to check on project deadlines coming up and those will be worked on also. They make sure any work that the developers have is completed and is working as expected from the point of view of an end user for the site making sure there are no issues with functionality/designs/user experience on the site. This is done by working through a predefined acceptance criteria if working on a support ticket or based on the ticket description or project specification if working on a project.

A Day In The Life of a QA Tester at a Software Development Company (2022) Youtube. Silverchip Ltd. Available at: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ChhYCujkMZ0 (Accessed: January 2, 2023)

QA tester:

The first task of the day is to check if the build and the deploy of the application went well, without any issues or errors. Sometimes they run their builds and deployment by themselves, depending about the builds. The second task of the day is reading emails (bug reports, documentation). From those emails and bug reports, it is determined what is a priority to test. Every task that is assigned needs to be done according in order of priority. Meetings occur daily, and discussions about issues and bug reports are part of daily routine. One of the biggest challenges of a QA tester is not having enough time. You can find many bugs in a full day of testing just one part of an app and become occupied trying to fix it. Another challenge is communicating to the developers why you think a real user is going to behave in a way that is different from what they’ve pictured.

Selih, J. (2023) “A Day in the Life of a QA Tester,” PanonIT, 15 April.
Available at: https://panonit.com/blog/day-life-qa-tester (Accessed: January 13, 2023).

Software engineer:

The day starts with logging into the projects they’re working on, checking their status and planning the day. This also includes supporting other team members or doing code-reviews. In the morning, teams usually have their daily stand-ups, where the progress of the projects are discussed, including any issues that need solving. A big challenge is trying to reach perfection whilst also being realistic. Trying to be perfect can lead to working too many hours trying to tweak things no one else would notice.

Kloth, R. (2020) “Interview: The Life Of A Software Engineer,” Mediaan, 27 August. Available at: https://mediaan.com/mediaan-blog/life-of-software-engineer (Accessed: January 11, 2023).

Data scientist:

typical day usually includes meetings with the analytics team to discuss the tasks of the day and brainstorm for possible solutions. Then they will start working on the data. Analysing data consists of three main tasks: gathering the data, cleaning the data, and finally processing the data. Gathering data is usually the easiest part. Cleaning the data means going through the data and trying to understand it, making corrections where needed such as moving outliers or data that should not be included. This step can take a lot of time, but understanding the data is crucial to start processing it. The data processing part is where you use programming skills to analyse the work and come up with solutions for the problem.

Taylor, T. (2022) “A Day In The Life Of A Data Analyst—What's It Really Like?,” Career Foundry, 30 November. Available at: https://careerfoundry.com/en/blog/data-analytics/whats-it-like-to-actually-be-a-data-analyst/ (Accessed: January 14, 2023).
